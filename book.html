Lament of the Kherson region

Tallinn-Kherson - MMXXIV

The book consists of four chapters representing four fundamental elements of the world: earth, water, fire, air.

Fire - the beginning of a full-scale invasion, the first days and months of the occupation.Battles for the region: the stories are full of terror and pain.

Earth - the period of occupation and liberation.

Stories about the unbreakable Ukrainian spirit and various forms of resistance to the invaders; stories about people, whose spirit is as strong as solid earth.

Water - the distruction of the Kakhovka HPP and its consequences.Stories about help and rescue, flood relief and adaptation.

Air - stories of the present and the recent past.Thoughts, hopes, expectations - all that keeps people living now. Uncertainty of the future.

them. Stories that need to be heard. 12 Ukrainian artists were inspired by the experiences recorded in the stories and, combining them with their own memories, created the artistic design of the book - illustrations and calligraphy.

Lamentation not only as a memory of dead, tortured, wounded and missing Ukrainians; of the destruction of houses, villages and cities, families; broken human destinies; of pain for all that was destroyed by the Russian aggressor, but also as something that gives strength to endure this sadness.

Lamentation as a form of addressing the world and recording the life of Kherson and the Kherson region today: life in all its manifestations - those that inspire hope and those that bring sadness.

The Kherson regional branch of the Sociological Association of Ukraine is launching the integration campaign "Kherson from everywhere". We start by creating a set of postcards about Kherson.

Viktor, Mylove: "We will share: 70 to 30"

In our local community, there were about 150 invaders, one company.

The commander liked our nature very much, boasted that from Mylivska to Novokiivska balka is now his territory, he will now oversee it. He created a harem of three girls. Got drunk in a bar once, tried to demonstrate the operation of the anti-aircraft system - a rocket fell on…

a nearby street, two women were injured. He told the farmers to sow the fields, demanded the enterprises to start working again: "We will divide: 70 to 30." When he was asked what he could do to help and what would he do to gain such a share, he answered: "I will make you passes to Simferopol, go, negotiate with the suppliers." But most people refused.

So, I decided to go and pick up our car. The route was through Zaporizhzhia. There is a large car market, it was turned into a checkpoint. Like a border crossing, only in Ukraine - between the free and the occupied territory. We formed a column, we are going. Ukrainian checkpoints every couple of kilometers. Maybe there were 7 or 10 of them. On the second or third a jam formed, everything stopped.

And then it starts exploding on the left, about four explosions. The bus is shaking so hard that it is about to tip over. We understand that if the missile was fired from the left side, then it will hit on the right side. And the column is standing.

Piece of newspaper: What to do during and after shelling During the shelling, if there was no alarm signal The remains of the MSLR rockets. They stick out from the ground, often not demined, can detonate at any time.

And rockets started hitting again. I look at all this, I pray to the Lord and think, do I really need that car? Why did I go? I should have stayed at home with my family. I think about our son, how he will grow up without me. We drove on, the driver is in a state of affect, squeezing everything he can out of the bus. We speed through all this gray area. Stopped at McDonald's, bought burgers and potatoes, cold Cola, to eat out the stress. Only then did I call home. My parents are crying, Ilona is crying. Someone told them that they saw me killed by a shell, but there was no connection. Oleksiy, Kakhovka

Leonid, Berislav: "Surprisingly, our gypsies helped"

I was very disappointed with the actions of our administration. They did not move a finger to help. Pharmacies were full of drugs, but they all closed on February 24. People appealed to the mayor to open them, the elderly people were without medicines. He said: It's private property, I can't do anything. Same with the food. Moreover, the head of our regional state administration only posted on Facebook on the 72nd day of the war: "I'm fine. Don't worry." Do you think this is normal? She was not seen or heard from for two and a half months. Do we also have to worry about her?

Surprisingly, our gypsies helped. They were the first to start supplying food, medicine, and cigarettes. It was the Roma community that took care of this, not the administration, not the city government. Of course, for money. But since shops and pharmacies are closed, everyone was in a panic, you couldn't buy anything at all, psychologically, it was major relief for people when they started importing medicines and grocery, even if they cost a lot of money.

Yana, Kherson: "This is the woman who comes for the paintings"

Just before the invasion, we were preparing an exhibition for the 50th anniversary of the Union of Artists. A serious event, our chairman said, "Bring your best work." Here comes February 24, of course, no one is thinking about the exhibition anymore, it is impossible to take the paintings back. I call the regional administration, they warn us not to even think of doing anything by ourselves. Then the occupation started.

I'm patient until mid-March, then I go to the Russian commandant's office and say: return the paintings. I went there once, twice, three, four times... They had three commandants changed during this time. Everyone says: "Why do you need these paintings? People are tortured, killed, and you bother us with your artists." For me, it was a way to overcome fear. I was very scared to go there, to endure half a day of interrogations. They already knew me there, they said: "This is the woman who comes for the paintings." While you wait, you will be asked about politics. "Are you an artist? Do you draw on military themes?” - “No. I am not interested in politics. I paint watermelons, pumpkins, plums, children, women, daisies”. Someone asks me: "Do you know an artist called Zozulia?" His works were also at the exhibition. I, naively, was very surprised, but I told everything, praised Zozulia as much as I could. The time has come to take away the paintings, someone is missing two paintings, someone is missing one. There is not a single painting of Zozulia.

Everything was stolen. There were a total of 80 paintings at the exhibition, 49 were returned. I ask: "Where are the rest of the works?" They answer, this is everything they had. When people with automatic weapons talk to you, you don't really argue.

Well, how can you defeat a city, where guys with a cast on their arms play basketball?

Tatyana, Kherson: "Do at least something, because everything is fenced off with nets!"

During the occupation, I was impressed by the large number of fish on the market during the spawning period - April and early May. Kherson was flooded with fish. Beryslav was filled with fish. Even on the websites, they wrote: “What is being done? The river is full of nets, no control, nobody does anything!”

I understand that in that state it was impossible to control or remove the nets. Especially if it is done by people with weapons, for example, or under their influence or under their “roof”. But there were complaints, I read these posts with appeals: "Do at least something, because everything is fenced off with nets!" It is one thing if people caught fish for food. But this is industrial fishing. They caught everything they could catch.

Karina, Kherson: "It's sad that I left them"

In Kherson at the beginning of the war, I was left alone and began to feed titmouse birds outside the window. I counted twenty-six of them. In the summer, they were no longer afraid to fly into my room. Now, when I meet titmouse in other cities, I remember my birds, and every time I feel sad that I left them. But I left not only my birds, and that makes me even sadder.

"I still haven't come to terms with my new life"

At the age of thirty, I realized that I wanted to live in Kherson, and for my family to live here. That's why we invested everything in real estate in Kherson, in repairs, etc. And then - kaboom, and the war started. And now I am in Georgia. We are thinking of moving to Moldova to be closer to Ukraine.

I live as if in a fog. I have not come to terms with my new life, I can't see myself anywhere but in Kherson. Furthermore, I do not believe in any victories. I think it will last long. But somewhere deep in my soul, I hope that we will return. As long as we have a place to return to. I'm not planning anything. I do not put down roots anywhere. I just live. It is very difficult to be alone with the little one, and on the other hand, she distracts me from an even greater depression, which would definitely get to me, if I would start thinking about the future, about Kherson and the people there. So I get distracted by everyday life, tantrums and some other strange life.

Once I was in a cast for six months, then it was also difficult, and I thought that nothing good would happen in my life, but then I somehow managed to get out. I think we will break through now. It can't last forever. Despite everything, we are alive - both the city and us.

Myroslav, Kherson: "Who would have thought!"

At the beginning of the war, I thought it was a good thing that my house was not near the route along which the orcs came.
During the occupation - it's a good thing that it was not near the places of their deployment and the Antonivka bridge. Who would have thought that 15 years ago, when I was building a house, I also should have calculated at what height I live from the standpoint of the level of "possible flooding".

Now my house is half underwater. So, it turns out that: If you live next door to Russians, you have to take it into account with almost every decision you make. Where to build a house, how to do business, etc. Fucking Russians!

Maria, Kherson: "After that, He is not running anywhere anymore"

When the shelling starts, I immediately want to hide somewhere inside. But my husband, on the contrary, if there is an explosion, he immediately runs to look outside. He says: "I'm not afraid for myself, God protects me. I'm worried about the car."

There is no garage, the car is parked on the street, the alarm is constantly activated at night. I can't sleep at all because of it. This is a permanent disco. And recently he ran like that and got concussed by an explosion. Didn't hear anything all day. And after that it stopped. He is not running anywhere anymore.

Kherson Nadia Biletska

"I hung up. What can you prove"

My aunt lives in Russia, in the city of Shakhty, Rostov region, I spoke with her. Their propaganda there is totally unhealthy. I talked to her on the phone, she asks: "How are you doing? What is happening there?" I tell her: "Everything is bad. Bombing. The battles are terrible. Kozatske village is no more."

And she says to me: "What are you talking about? Our Russians don't bomb." I hung up. What can you prove to her? She is an elderly person, watches TV all the time. It is not enough for her that her nephew says: "Kozatske was bombed to the ground. There is no Kozatske." She does not believe us. What else do I need to do, swear on God's name?

Petro, Kakhovka

"They took out her brother's crushed bones from her head"

We delivered insulin and other medicines to Kherson through the so-called hubs of the Mykolaiv and Zaporizhia regions. We left the occupied territory, took medicine, returned - the road sometimes took several days. Thirty block posts in one direction.

In our volunteer community, we exchanged information. When you see that there is no passage, it is very important to warn your colleagues. Where it is faster to drive, where it is safer. The word "safer", however, can be put in quotation marks.

Anton Kushnir drove like this from Kherson to Mykolaiv, accompanied the evacuation bus, drove through all the roadblocks, stopped, asked his sister, who was sitting next to him, to take the phone and look at the map, how to go further. She bends over to the glove box, there’s a shot, a large-caliber bullet hits Anton in the head. The force of the blow was so strong that the sister was injured by fragments of his skull.

The doctors performed an operation and removed the crushed bones of her brother from her head. They asked the Russians to hand over his body, they have only one old tune: "You won't get anything, it was done by the Ukrainian Nazis." They burned the car in order not to conduct an investigation. It is said that Anton was buried by the residents of the village of Lyubomirivka near the place where he died, but this is still not clear.

"To hell with them, let's go around through the dirt road"

We left the occupation at the end of April, we hired driver called Serhiy who was recommended. He took us and another family from Kherson. We left with him in the morning, passed almost all the roadblocks, until we stopped at one of them with all the other cars. The invaders say, turn around: "We won't let anyone until the end of May hollydays."

It must be said here that the drivers who were involved in the evacuation are very brave and adventurous guys. Serhii says: "To hell with them, let's go around through the dirt road." Turned off the headlights and drove through the fields and gardens. At night, we stopped in some dark village, asked to sleep in a house, tried to sleep a little, and at 5 in the morning we set off. At the first checkpoint, a sleepy soldier came out and asked: why so early. Serhii answered that…

we were sent this way from the previous checkpoint. Then we drove up to the second checkpoint - there was no one there. The driver tells me: "Quietly, without knocking the door, get out and lift the barrier." I unscrew the wire on the barrier and think, lets hope nobody uncharges an AK at me at this point. The next checkpoint was already Ukrainian and the military looked at us very surprised. A day before we came (and three more days after we passed), the invaders did not let a single car pass. That's how we got out of the occupation.

Georgy, Kherson: "Isn't there another place for rest?"

I was in the regional hospital for an examination, there were three men sitting near the reception room with injuries of various degrees and the nurse was shouting at them. I eavesdropped... It turns out that they went to dacha fishing and came under fire. Some women from their company even died, she was torn to pieces... Is it possible to be more stupid? Is there no other place to rest? There is a war! Go to where hell is! Not only do they risk their lives, but they also endanger the doctors, who go to take them from the shelling.

Oleg, Kherson: "They asked to bring 5 million hryvnias for our pensioners"

A normal evacuation from Kherson looked like this. We formed a column of 2-3 Red Cross buses. Sometimes other volunteers also joined us. They took the children, wives of soldiers of ATO, often with forged documents, because then there were already lists and people could be taken. Of course, we drove through Oleshki, at first there were 25 checkpoints, then a few more were added. Block posts with soldiers are not so bad, but LPR and DPR soldiers are terrible, they are dirt, shit, not people. Dirty, stand there, begging: give batteries, give flashlights, give tomatoes. Why doesn’t the second army of the world provide for you? It was disgusting to watch. The Russians themselves considered them vagrants.

The most nightmarish block-post was the last one, counting from Kherson. FSB snipers were standing there. Unload everything, then load it again. Insulin was not allowed to be brought in, although we did so anyway. Once we even transported money in a box for bananas. People came to me in Zaporizhzhia and said that we need to bring 5 million hryvnias for our pensioners to the occupied territory. Payments do not go through, people are without pensions. None of my colleagues knew, I only confessed to them later, when Ihanded over the money at Ukrposhta. Our people shuddered. That box of bananas was lying in the most prominent place in the bus, no one paid attention to it.

Anastasia, Kherson: "Couldn't anyone visit her at least once every few days?"

I have been living in Germany for a long time. At the end of winter, I came to pick up her old mother from Kherson, Shumenskyi microdistrict. In the city, where there are food kits, humanitarian headquarters work, an elderly lady died a terrible death!

You feel bad not just for people, even for dogs. While I was there, I fed them every day, took canned food to them in the yard. I could not just let it go. Their owners abandoned them, they left, and the dogs were left behind. They run in packs. And how it happened that a person died of hunger, I can't even imagine. Couldn't anyone visit her at least once every few days?!

I live in Kherson near Korabeliv Square. I asked a friend to send me a power bank by Nova Poshta. She looked at the map, where I live, and sent it to the nearest NP branch. And it was on Ostriv.

Yuriy, Kherson: "What, they raped you too?!"

The owner of a flower shop says: "I'm making the bride's bouquet. This is not the first wedding this month, and I am very happy about it."

This case of mass rape in the coastal villages became known thanks to the Russian military police from Nova Kakhovka, who came to investigate the case. Locals were questioned. Locals told everything: who, how, where. Then this military police came to the headquarters to ask the Russian servicemen if they knew.
