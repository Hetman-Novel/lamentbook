About project

The "Lament of Kherson region" project is the creation of a unique Book of stories of the inhabitants of the Kherson region, which was produced using the technologies of the 10th century. Ukrainian, in particular, Kherson artists during a 10-day residency in Tallinn, Estonia, under the mentorship of Mr. Anatoly Lyutyuk.

From the first days of the war until today, Kherson Oblast has been partially occupied, already free Kherson and the right bank are under attack from the enemy.

Sudden occupation, daily shelling, blowing up of the Kakhovskaya HPP dam, basements and torture, killing of civilians, including children and animals, are only a part of what is actually happening in Kherson region. Destroyed libraries, burned books, exported cultural values, killed cultural figures, the imposition of the Russian language and culture on the occupied territories, and alongside these horrors - ordinary life: trying to survive, work, teach children in Ukrainian schools, help the Armed Forces and sometimes try rest, so as not to go crazy.

The stories of people who experienced and continue to experience these events were collected by us and included in the great Book "Lament of the Kherson Region".

The Lament of the Kherson region is a unique book that consists of 52 stories of the residents of the Kherson region, illustrated by artists during a 10-day residency in Tallinn, Estonia, under the mentorship of the legendary man Anatoly Lyutyuk.

Before starting the residency, the project team conducted long and painstaking work.
First, it is a collection of stories. Kherson sociologists under the leadership of the project producer, sociologist Mykola Homanyuk collected about 70 documentary stories told to them by ordinary people, residents of the Kherson region. The names of the characters in the stories have been changed for security reasons.

Simultaneously with the collection of stories, the curator of the project from Ukraine, Volodymyr Reinhart, held an open call, where any artist could submit his portfolio and be selected for the residency. During the selection, priority was given, of course, to artists from the Kherson region.

Meanwhile, preparations were being made in Estonia for the arrival of the guests: drawing paper and ink were being prepared and the general concept of working with artists was being developed.

The main event of the project is a 10-day residency, during which the illustrations for the collected stories were created.
10 artists selected during the open call came to Tallinn for 10 days to work together on the book. Along with them, Volodymyr Reinhart, project curator from Ukraine, and Mykola Homanyuk, project producer, took part in creating illustrations for the book.

He transferred his calligraphy and design experience to artists during master classes conducted by the center's best specialists.

During the residency, the participants attended master classes in calligraphy, paper making, letterpress printing, got acquainted with the basic principles of calligraphy and design of this type of books, and applied this knowledge in practice.

In addition to working on the book, the artists took part in the "Stand with Ukraine" event on the second anniversary of the war, and went on city tours conducted for them by Tetiana Stetsko, a native of Kherson who now lives in Estonia.

In 10 days, the artists made illustrations and calligraphy for 52 stories, creating 11 chapters of their own in the book. Each of these sections was created by an artist in his own style with his own concept, adding his own thoughts and concerns in the illustration.

For example, Maria Apryatova, who is engaged in encyclopedic watercolors on the subject of animals and plants, selected stories related to them in one way or another, and Marianna Tarish, who spent the first weeks of the occupation in Kherson, in the Ostriv microdistrict, made a scope the main theme of her illustrations. target as a symbol of how she felt at that time.

The last chapter of the book - Herbarium, consists of Kherson plants, artifacts: advertisements, wrappers, newspapers, other documents collected during and after the occupation. These artifacts were collected by artists who came directly from Kherson. Most of the documents are from Mykola Homanyuk's personal collection. The plants were collected thanks to Oleksandr Kudykinskyi, a friend from Kherson, and the SAU HOV.

Kherson reed was brought to us by Maria Apryatova from the Odessa coast, it came there with the flow of water after the Kakhovskaya HPP dam was blown up.

After the residency was completed, 12 separate chapters of the book, which have not yet been combined into a single whole, could be seen by Estonians and Ukrainian refugees in Estonia at an exhibition organized by the Center of Ukrainian Culture.

At the opening of the exhibition, Ukrainian ambassador to Estonia Maksym Kononenko and Monika Haukanimm, head of the central district of Tallinn, were present.

Work on the book has not been completed.

After the departure of the artists from Tallinn, work continued on creating the first letters, bookmarks and book cover.

The stories for the bookmarks of the book were selected from among the posts of the Kherson TUT Telegram channel and were written by refugees from the Kherson Region in Tallinn.

The calligraphy of the book title and first letters belongs to Olga Strutskaya. After its creation in electronic format, the files were transferred to a photopolymer cliché and printed with letterpress, in its modernized version.

Anatoliy Lyutiuk worked on the introligation (cover and creation of the book binding).

The cover is made of wood, decorated with a fragment of a projectile, which was found in the yard of her house by Valeryia Guran, an artist from Kherson.

The total weight of the book was 6 kilograms.
